Documented Death Toll in Ukraine Tops 61,000

By Coralie Carlson
Dec. 15, 2023

More than 61,000 Ukrainians have died in the war since Russia’s full-fledged invasion began nearly 22 months ago, according to the Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project, an independent group that records documented cases of political violence and protests worldwide.

More than a third of the deaths were recorded in Donets'k Oblast, or province, which includes the devastated city of Mariupol.

Sabra Ayres, the editor at large for the Reckoning Project, a non-profit organization documenting war crimes in Ukraine, points out that the exact number of casualties in the war is unknown. Neither the Ukrainian government nor the military has released official death tolls.

Ayres says that humanitarian groups estimate the death toll including civilians could be 100,000 or more.

The Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project records casualties that have been documented in the media or by others on the ground.

“Since nobody is releasing official figures, and it’s difficult to know what those figures would be with bodies still lying in fields, this method provides a best guess to the actual death toll,” Ayres said.
